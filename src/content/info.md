RikiTraki is a web app for viewing your GPS tracks and photos on a 3D globe. It gives you a simple personal space for your own data and a way to browse public tracks from others. The goal is to keep things clear, lightweight, and easy to navigate.

### How It Works

RikiTraki is built around a **search‑center–based architecture**. Instead of loading everything at once, the map fetches tracks dynamically based on your chosen search center — whether that’s where you are or where you want to focus. This keeps the experience fast and scalable, even as the dataset grows.

Behind the scenes, RikiTraki automatically:

- detects regions and locations from GPS data  
- aligns tracks to terrain  
- organizes photos by time and position  
- keeps your personal and global views separate, so your own tracks and photos live in a clean, private space that works like a lightweight logbook on top of the globe

### What’s New in 2.0

RikiTraki 2.0 is a complete rebuild with a modern backend and a smoother, faster UI.

Highlights include:

- a unified Cesium‑powered 3D map — the old 2D/3D switching is gone, making navigation smoother and more intuitive  
- personal */username* spaces  
- improved track and photo uploads  
- automatic region detection  
- terrain‑aligned rendering  
- better performance on mobile and desktop  

This is still a Beta release — features will continue to evolve.
